Nil Biescas

I am a first-year Master’s student in Data Science at EPFL (Switzerland). I graduated in the top 3% of Spain’s first Artificial Intelligence cohort at the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona (UAB).

I am currently a Research Intern at the Visual Intelligence and Learning Lab (VILAB) at EPFL, advised by Professor Amir Zamir, where I develop vision tokenizers for 3D understanding with robotics applications.

Previously, I conducted research at the Computer Vision Center (CVC) and completed my BSc thesis at Technical University of Munich (TUM) under Daniel Cremers. I have publications at ECCV and ICDAR and am deeply motivated to advance physical AI and multimodal learning.

Selected Research

Haptic Teleoperation and Learning from Demonstration with the Franka Robotic Arm Manuscript, 2025

We teleoperated a Franka Panda robot using a Falcon haptic device through ROS, exploring GMM/GMR and Task-Parameterized GMM for trajectory learning and generalization.

AnyCam-3D: Extending AnyCam for Dense 3D Geometry Reconstruction in Dynamic, Uncalibrated Videos BSc Thesis, TUM (Supervised by Daniel Cremers), 2025

Expanding AnyCam to predict dense point clouds of dynamic scenes from uncalibrated video sequences.

GeoContrastNet: Contrastive Key-Value Edge Learning for Language-Agnostic Document Understanding Nil Biescas, Carlos Boned, Josep Lladós, Sanket Biswas ICDAR (Oral Presentation), 2024

A two-stage graph attention framework for structured document understanding without relying on OCR, capturing spatial relations via geometric edge features.

LayeredDoc: Domain Adaptive Document Restoration with a Layer Separation Approach Nil Biescas, Maria Pilligua, Javier Vazquez-Corral, Josep Lladós, Ernest Valveny, Sanket Biswas ECCV WiCV & ICDAR ADAPDA, 2024

Decomposing documents into semantic layers to improve domain adaptation for restoration tasks.
